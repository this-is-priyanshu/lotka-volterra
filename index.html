Lotka-Volterra model


The Lotka-Volterra equations, also known as the predator-prey equations, are a pair of first-order non-linear differential equations.

The equations are often used to describe population dynamics between two species in a habitat, one being the predator and the other being the prey.

Prey equation: dx/dt = αx - βxy

Predator equation: dy/dt = δxy - γy

In the above equations, x is the prey population and y is the predator population.

dx/dt and dy/dt represent the rate of change of the prey and predator populations respectively.

α, β, δ, γ are positive real parameters which describe the interactions between the two species.
